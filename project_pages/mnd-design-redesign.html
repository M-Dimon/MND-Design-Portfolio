Re-design of a website

For the first project, we were tasked with redesigning a website of our choosing, so long as it appeared to need a serious makeover. The website my group found was http://www.dansk-international-noedhjaelp.dk/ (Dansk International Nødhjælp), a website by volunteers to help to provide aide to the eastern European countries.

Although informative and functioning, the website itself didn't make proper use of design principles and therefore appeared messy at times. As a result, we decided to brainstorm ideas about what to and how to incorporate elements, along with what to keep and with how to keep it.

Part 1 - Brainstorm
During the brainstorm, we decided to minimize the layout and menu while maintaining the information found on the site. This was to create a user-friendly website, with a high readability count, without removing the necessary pages found on the website. We categorized and grouped them, which resulted in the need for four pages, each with access to relevant subpages, making it easier to navigate the website.

After we did the menu planning, we looked at the overall layout to determine what to keep, which to alter, and figure out which color palette to use. As a result, we decided to use a simple color palette, using blue, black, white, and grey. This was inspired both by the website itself and the research we did into other international aid organizations. We also decided to redesign the logo and give it a fixed position to help brand the website, give it familiarity with its viewers, and change the overall layout for the pages. Hence, readability and user-friendliness become vital features.

Part 3 - Adding details & finishing the prototype.
After the wireframe was done, we worked on the different sliders we wanted to incorporate, added a hover effect on buttons and the map found on the landing page.

After that, we ensured that every button on the page functioned. Every bit of information was incorporated and double-checked. Every needed element was working as it should before we moved on to writing the report content.

While making the product, we continuously wrote and designed the report, and layout for the report, using the same color palette as our product, ensuring a red tread between product and report.

Here we decided how to set up the different pages, where text boxes should go, what they should look like, etc. We decided to keep the overall layout simple while incorporating various elements and pictures of relevance and specific minor details to add some flair to the report.
